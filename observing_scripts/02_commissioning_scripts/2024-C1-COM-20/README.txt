PLEASE READ
==========

Observations of Pallas.  This is an unresolved asteroid.

Source: Pallas

On nights when no HWP is installed, we should use the script for Stokes I.  

On nights when the HWP is installed and spinning, we should use the scripts for StokesQU.

Stokes I: 
---------

Pattern type: 5 min 3x3 Lissajous mapping pattern  

File: A script should be made that is analogous to target_3C286_Stokes_I.lmtot in COM-09

Execute this file 30 times.  

Note that it is very important for the Stokes I maps to have the observations covering as wide a range of elevations as possible.  See "availability" below for suggestions on this.  

Total time: ~ 30 x 5 ~ 2 h 30 m

Stokes Q/U:
-----------

3x3 rasters with 5 arcsec/s mapping velocity and 0.23 arcmin spacing 
(~9 min give or take depending on turnaround time)

Files:

Scripts should be made that are analogous to the following six scripts (in COM-09): 

target_3C286_QU_rot0_processed.lmtot
target_3C286_QU_rot30_processed.lmtot
target_3C286_QU_rot60_processed.lmtot
target_3C286_QU_rot90_processed.lmtot
target_3C286_QU_rot120_processed.lmtot
target_3C286_QU_rot150_processed.lmtot

These 6 scripts correspond to 6 different rotations: 0, 30, 60, 90, 120, and 150

Execute each of them 1 time, the repeat the 0, 60, and 120 degree rotations, for a total of 9 executions.

Note that it is NOT important for the Stokes QU maps to have the observations covering a wide a range of elevations.

Total time: ~ 9 x 9 ~ 1 h 20 m


Availability: 
-----------

On April 26th, Pallas is available between 4:40 UT and 8:00 UT and again between 9:40 UT and sunrise.  For the Stokes I observations where a wide range of elevations is required, we suggest observing continuously between 9:40 UT and sunrise might be a good option.  Alternatively, observations at the beginning and end of the 4:40 UT to 8:00 UT interval.
